No respectable Swede would post a list of recipes without including one of glögg. This one came to us via Anders Q. . . .
 Wine Glögg with a Kick

  • 3/4 cup brandy
  • 2 cinnamon sticks, whole
  • 15 cloves, whole
  • 1 piece ginger, whole & dry
  • 1 tsp cardamom, cracked
  • 1/4 orange peel
  • 1 bottle red wine (Experiment with different types of wine. We buy an inexpensive wine that is not too dry and does not have a lot of tannin. Try a Sweet Red type.)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • raisins and sliced almonds

Combine brandy and spices in a glass jar with lid; let stand for a day, occasionally mixing the content. Strain the spices and combine the brandy with sugar and wine; stir until sugar is dissolved. Refrigerate. When serving: Heat until warm but not boiling; combine 1/2 cup of glögg with 1 Tbsp raisins and 1 Tbsp almonds. Serve with gingersnaps cookies. You cannot get anymore Swedish than this. (8 servings. Keeps for several weeks in refrigerator.)

Because you can make this glögg very quickly, we call this recipe . . .
 Glögg Lightning

  • 1 1/2 oz red wine (Barefood, Sweet Red works very well)
  • 1 oz Long Island iced tea liquor mix (equal parts of rum, vodka, tequila, gin, triple sec)
  • 1/2 oz Triple Sec
  • dash of ground allspice

Mix all ingredients in small mug. Heat in microwave until piping hot (30 sec.). Add sugar, raisins, and almonds if you like. Serve with gingersnaps cookies. (1 serving)

When we are on a low carb diet and would like to have a drink, here are some that works pretty well for us. . . .
 Low-carb (and low calorie) Drinks

  • 3 pkg Equal sugar substitute
  • 3 pkg Splenda sugar substitute
  • 4 1/2 oz lime juice
  • 3 oz orange juice
  • about 4 1/2 oz vodka
  • diet cranberry juice
  • diet coke
  • vodka
  • gin
  • white rum
  • tequila
  • cognac (or brandy)

Mix the first four ingredients in a 12-oz bottle. Add vodka to fill bottle and store the base in the refrigerator.
For a Margarita, shake 2 oz of base and 1.5 oz tequila with crushed ice and pour into a salt-rimmed martini glass.
For a Cosmopolitan, stir 1.75 oz of base, 1 oz cranberry juice, and 2 oz of vodka with ice and strain into a martini glass.
For a White Lady, shake 1.75 oz of base and 2 oz of gin with crushed ice and strain into a martini glass.
For a Sidecar, shake 2 oz of base and 2 oz of cognac with ice and strain into a martini glass.
For a Long Island iced tea, pour 1 oz base, 0.5 oz rum, 0.5 oz tequila, 0.5 oz gin, 0.5 oz vodka, and 0.5 oz of diet coke over ice. Dilute with 0.5-1 oz water if too strong. (If you find the mix to tart, substitute some lime juice with water.)

One day we ran out of club soda and came up with. . . .
 Ginger Mojito

  • 12 mint leaves
  • 1 sugar cube
  • 1/2 Tbsp fresh lime juice
  • 1 1/2 oz white rum
  • 1/2 oz simple syrup (1 part sugar, 1 part boiling water)
  • 2 oz good ginger beer (or ginger ale)

Muddle mint leaves, sugar cube, and lime juice. Shake with the ice, rum, and simple sugar. Add to glass and top with ginger beer. Stir to mix.

While cruising on NCL, we really liked one of their martini drinks. Here is our version of. . . .
 Lavender Patch Martini

  • 3/4 oz fresh lemon or lime juice
  • 1/4 oz simple syrup
  • 3/4 oz lavender syrup
  • 1 oz pineapple juice
  • 2 1/2 oz vodka or gin
Shake with ice and strain into martini glass. Add mint leaf for decoration and added fragrance. If you want, substitute the simple syrup with pineapple habanero syrup for a spicy version.

With a slight modification from a traditional recipe, here is our version of. . . .
 Orange Chocolate Martini

  • 1 1/2 oz Irish Cream (e.g., Bailey's)
  • 1 oz creme de cacao (e.g., Leroux)
  • 1/2 oz triple sec
  • 1 1/2 oz vodka
Shake with ice and strain into martini glass. If you think it is too strong, add 1 1/2 oz of chocolate milk. if you want to try something different, use our lavender syrup instead of Triple Sec for a Lavender Chocolate Martini. For another variation, substitute a good quality Eggnog for the Irish Cream and use white rum instead of vodka. It makes for a great Holiday drink. Please note that this is a alcoholic drink, masked with cream and sweetness...drink with care.

While the Sazerac that you order at the Sazerac Bar in New Orleans is a little complicated to make, there is a simple alternative. . .
 Simple Sazerac

  • 3 dashes of Peychaud's Aromatic Bitters
  • 3 dashes of Angostura Aromatic Bitters
  • 5 drops of Absinthe
  • 3/4 oz simple syrup (you can substitute some with Triple Sec)
  • 3 oz Sazerac Rye Whiskey
Stir with a lot of ice and strain into rock glass. Add orange peel for decoration and added fragrance, or obmit if you added the Triple Sec.

 Riptide

  • 3/4 oz water
  • 3/4 oz lime juice
  • 3/4 oz habanero pineapple syrup
  • 3/4 oz hibiscus syrup
  • 2 oz gold (or white) rum
Shake with ice and serve with ice. If you want it stronger, substitute the water with rum.

This is an obvious twist to one of the above drinks. Here is. . .
 Mint Chocolate Martini

  • 10-12 mint leaves
  • 1/2 oz simple sugar
  • 1 1/2 oz Irish Cream (e.g., Bailey's)
  • 1 oz creme de cacao (e.g., Leroux)
  • 1 1/2 oz vodka
Muddle the mint leaves with the simple sugar. Shake with the rest of the ingredients and ice. Strain into martini glass. As before, if you think it is too strong, add 1 1/2 oz of chocolate milk.
