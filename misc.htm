 (Salt-free) Creole Seasoning

  • 2 1/2 Tbsp paprika
  • 2 Tbsp garlic powder
  • 2 Tbsp black pepper, ground
  • 1 Tbsp onion powder
  • 1 Tbsp Cayenne pepper
  • 1 Tbsp dried oregano
  • 1 Tbsp dried thyme

Mix all ingredients well and store in closed container. This will make a very good salt-free Creole seasoning. If the recipe does not call for salt-free, add 1 part of salt for every 4-5 parts of salt-free.

 Pineapple Habanero Syrup

  • 1 8-oz can of crushed pinaapple
  • 1/2-1 seeded habanero pepper
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup water

Heat all the ingredients in a saucepan over low-medium to a boil. Using a sturdy spoon or something else, press repeatedly the habanero to the bottom of the pan. Simmer for 5 min and allow to cool. Strain through colander and use the spoon to press through any remaining liquid. After you remove the habanero pieces (or chop them finely), you can save the pineapple mash for jam that works great with Brie cheese. (Makes 8 oz. Substitute 0.5 oz of simple sugar with this syrup in your favorite cocktail for a little kick.)

 Lavender Syrup

  • 1 cup water
  • 3 Tblsp dried lavender flowers (food grade)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • lime juice

Add water and lavender to sauce pan. Boil for 2 min, remove from heat and strain. Stir in sugar until dissoved and bottle. If you think the color is booring, add lime juice drop-wise until you get a nice red/light purple color.

 Cranberry Orange Relish

  • 12 oz fresh or frozen whole cranberries
  • zest from one orange, finely chopped
  • 1 orange peeled, seeded, and coursely chopped
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • dash of ground cinnamon

Batchwise chop cranberry and orange in food processor enough to be smooth but still a litte chunky. In bowl, mix the chopped fruit with the other ingredients. Placed in refrigerator over night before serving as side to your holiday meal. (10 servings)
