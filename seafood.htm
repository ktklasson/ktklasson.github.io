 Hot Fish

  • 4 8-oz fish fillets
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil
  • 1 Tbsp flour
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp white pepper
  • 1/2 cup white wine (cooking wine is OK)
  • 4 Tbsp parsley, freshly chopped
  • 1/2 Tbsp red pepper flakes

In skillet, cook (but don't burn) garlic in oil. Add flour, salt, pepper, and wine. Boil for a couple of minutes. Add one cup of water, and bring to a boil before adding fish, parsley, and pepper flakes. Cover and simmer for 10 min. Serve with cooked rice or cauliflower rice. (4 servings)

 Crab Quiche

  • 9-inch pastry shell
  • 2 green onions,sliced
  • 1 Tbsp butter
  • 1 8-oz can crab meat, drained
  • 1 Tbsp flour
  • 1 1/2 cup Swiss cheese, shredded
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 cup half-&-half
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • dash of pepper
  • dash of nutmeg
  • dash of hot sauce

Bake pastry shell for 10 min at 350°F. Saute onion in butter for about 2 min. Add crab meat and flour; set aside. Put half of the cheese on the bottom of the pastry shell, then all the crab mixture, and the rest of the cheese. Quickly mix eggs, half-&-half, and spices; pour into shell. Bake at 350°F for 30-40 min. until lightly brown and toothpick comes out clean when inserted the center. Let the quiche rest for 10 min. and serve with a Cesar salad. If you do not like crab meat, substitute julienned ham instead (6 servings)

 Janssons Temptation

  • 1.5 lb raw potatoes (peeled and julienned)
  • 3 small/medium yellow onions (diced)
  • 4 2-oz cans of anchovies in olive oil (drained)
  • 3-4 cups of heavy (30%) cream (if you feel this is too much, substitute with half-and-half)
  • 3 Tbsp of plain bread crumbs
  • 2 Tbsp butter (in thin pieces)

Arrange onion at the bottom of 9"x18" pan. Separate the anchovies into thin fillets, divide each into three pieces, and place evenly on top of the onion. Add potatoes on top. Press down on the potatoes to make it a bit denser. Pour enough cream over the top until the potatoes are almost (but not completely) covered. Sprinkle the bread crumbs on top and dab with the butter. Place in middle of pre-heated oven at 425°F for 50-55 min. Let it rest for 20 minutes before serving. (Can be cooked in advance and reheated in 275°F oven.) (6-8 servings)

 Poached Fish with Cranberry Hollandaise

  • 1/4 cup white wine
  • 1/2 lemon, sliced
  • 6 black peppercorns, whole
  • 4 sprigs of parsley
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 4 fish fillets, 6-8 oz each
  • 1 cup cranberry juice, boiled down to 1/4 cup
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 2 sicks butter, softened
  • 1/8 tsp Cayenne pepper

In large skillet (not cast iron), add 1/2 cup of water and bring to a simmer. Add wine, lemon, peppercorns, parsley, and salt. Cook for 5 min. Meanwhile get sauce started. In a metal bowl set over a saucepan of simmering water, whisk the eggs yolks until the change to a pale yellow color. Wisk in the softened butter, a tablespoon at a time. If the sauce shows any sign of curdling, remove the bowl from the saucepan and keep whisking until it cools. Do this until half the butter is gone, then add the reduced cranberry juice and one Tbsp of water from the fish pan. Whisk in the rest of the butter until fluffy. Wisk in the Cayenne and set the sauce aside. Back to the pan of water: with the liquid lighly simmering, add the fish in the pan and cook for 6-10 min, depending on thickness. Remove the fish with a slotted spoon and serve immedeately topped with sauce and mild (in flavor) steamed or boiled vegetables (e.g., cauliflower or potatoes). (4 servings)

 Orange Fish

  • 4 fish fillets, 6-8 oz each
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • 3 green onions, sliced
  • 1 Tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil (plus 2 Tbsp more for frying)
  • 1/4 tsp ginger, ground
  • 1/8 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup flour
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/8 tsp Cayenne pepper

Arrange fish in a baking dish. Mix the next six ingredients in a bowl and pour over the fish. Cover and marinate in refrigerator for 30 min. Combine flour, salt, and Cayenne in shallow dish. Drain fish, reserving marinade. Dip fish in flour mixture. Pan fry fish in 2 Tbsp oil in skillet, turning once. Transfer fish to platter and keep warm (e.g., in 250°F oven). Add marinade to skillet and simmer until volume reduces to half. Pour over fish and serve with mild (in flavor) steamed or boiled vegetables (e.g., cauliflower or potatoes). (4 servings)

 Fish and Corn Chowder

  • 2 lb of white fish; e.g., tilapia
  • 1 Tbsp salt-free Creole seasoning
  • 1 Tbsp butter
  • 1 cup onion, chopped
  • 1 cup celery, chopped
  • 2 Tbsp green onion, chopped (1/2 Tbsp dry)
  • 1 Tbsp garlic, chopped
  • 4 cups vegetable stock
  • 15 oz Montery Jack Queso dip
  • 2 Tbsp tartar sauce
  • 1/2 tsp dill weed
  • 1/2 tsp white pepper, ground
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 30 oz canned whole corn kernels, drained
  • 2 cups whipping cream

Season fish with the Creole seasoning and arrange on greased baking dish. Bake at 350°F until done (10-15 min) and allow to cool. Meanwhile, sautee onions, celery, and garlic in butter until soft. Add the vegetable stock and use blender stick to puree the mixture. Add dip, tartar sauce, and spices. Simmer for 10 min. Chop fish and add with corn. Simmer for 10 min. Add cream and heat until hot. (8 servings)

 Old Man's Mix

  • 4 hard-boiled eggs (peeled, chopped)
  • 1 medium red onion (diced)
  • 3 2-oz cans of anchovies in olive oil (drained, chopped)
  • 3 Tbsp sour cream
  • 3 Tbsp creme fraiche (or sour cream)
  • 3 Tbsp green onion (chopped)
  • 3 Tbsp fresh dill (chopped, or 1 Tbsp dry)
  • pinch of black pepper
  • lettuce leaf, more creme fraiche, and sliced rye bread for serving

Fold all the ingredients together (don't over-mix). Place in refrigerator for about 1 hr. To serve, use wedges of rye bread with a little creme fraiche, a piece of lettuce, and 2 Tbsp of mix. Perfect as appetizer. (10-12 servings)
