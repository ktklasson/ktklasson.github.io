Gingerbread cookies are very traditional in Sweden around Christmas time. The key is to roll them thin and to use high quality cutters in shapes of hearts, stars, clovers, circles, and pigs. Here is our American-kitchen-modified recipe for . . .
 Gingerbread Cookies

  • 1 cup dark brown sugar, packed
  • 3/4 cup butter, softened
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup dark corn syrup
  • 2 tsp baking soda
  • 3 tsp allspice, ground (or 1.5 tsp ground cinnamon & 1.5 tsp ground cloves)
  • 1.5 tsp ginger, ground
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 4 cups flour

Combine the first eight ingredients and mix with electric mixer in large bowl. Continue mixing while adding in three cups of flour, one at a time. Use your hands to mix in the last cup of flour. Wrap in plastic and leave in refrigerator over night. Roll 1/8-inch thin on floured surface and make your cuts; transfer to greased cookie sheets and bake at 325°F for 6 to 8 minutes until the edges turn slightly brown. Do not over-bake, best-tasting cookies has about the same color on top as on the bottom. Serve with glögg. (About 5 dozen)

 June F.'s Garbage Cookies

  • 1 cup butter, softened
  • 2 cups dark brown sugar, packed
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 3 cups oatmeal
  • 2 cups flour
  • 1 cup pecans, chopped
  • 2 cups dates, chopped
  • 2 cups chocolate chips, semi-sweet

Combine the first six ingredients and mix with electric mixer in large bowl. Fold in the other ingredients in order. Let dough rest for a couple of hours. Form small round cookies (1.5 inches in diameter, 0.5 inch thick). Bake on greased cookie sheets at 350°F for 8 to 10 minutes. Serve with Swedish Gevalia coffee. (About 5 dozen)

 $500 Cookies

  • 1 box yellow cake mix
  • 1 lb powdered sugar
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 stick (8 Tbsp) margarine

Heat oven to 350°F. Press cake mix evenly into the bottom of 11" x 16" pan. Cut margarine into thin squares and spread on top of cake mix. Mix sugar, cream cheese, and eggs until smooth. Pour mixture into pan. Bake for 40 min, let the pan cool, and cut into squares. Serve with milk or coffee. (40 squares)

 Coconut Balls

  • 1 stick butter, softened at room temperature
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups traditional oats (for breakfast oat meal)
  • 2 1/4 Tbsp cocoa powder (usually a Dutch brand)
  • 1 1/2 tsp vanilla sugar (hard to find, check Whole Foods) dissolved in
  • 2 1/4 Tbsp orange juice or cold strong coffee
  • coconut flakes for outside coating

Mix all ingredients by hand with a wooden spoon or spatula in the order above. Dont move to the next ingredient until the previous ones have been mixed; e.g., when you have mixed the butter and sugar, add the oats and mix until uniform before you add the cocoa, etc.
Spread a deep layer of coconut flakes in a large plate. Shape a Tbsp of mix in your hand to a ball. Roll the ball in the coconut flakes until you have a nice coating. Place the finished coconut balls on a clean plate. You will get messy and if you feel that the mix is too soft, you can put the mix in the fridge for 10 minutes. Once done, let the balls rest in the fridge over night before serving them chilled.
If you can not find the vanilla sugar, you can substitute with vanilla extract (1 tsp)just remember to reduce the other liquid amount to just under 2 Tbsp. (20-22 balls)

 Bittersweet Chocolate Bark

  • 1 lb bittersweet chocolate, chopped
  • 1/2 cup unsalted roasted pistachios (or mixed nuts)
  • 1/2 tsp coarse sea salt

Line a cookie sheet with parchment paper. In double boiler set over simmering water, heat the chocolate until two-thirds is melted. Remove from heat and stir until the chocolate is completely melted. Work fast and spread the warm chocolate on the parchment paper. Scatter the nuts and salt. Gently tap the cookie sheet on the work surface to flatten the chocolate and allow toppings to sink in slightly. Refrigerate for about 15 min. Cut or break the bark into 2-inch pieces and serve.

 Candied Almonds

  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 1/2 tsp cinnamon, ground
  • 1/4 to 1/2 tsp Cayenne pepper, ground
  • 1 1/4 cups whole almonds

Heat water, sugar, and spices to go a boil and simmer for 15 min. Remove from heat. Add almonds and stir continuously until it gets sticky and hard to stir. Quickly spread out on parchment paper and separate the almonds from each other. Let them cool for 10 min and store in air-tight container.

 Cardamom Bread, Filled

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour, divided
  • 1/3 cup sugar
  • 2 tsp cardamom, ground
  • 1 tsp cinnamon, ground
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 1/2 pkg dry yeast
  • 5 1/2 Tbsp butter
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 egg, blended and divided
    Filling, mixed together:
  • 3 1/2 Tbsp butter, soften
  • 3 Tbsp sugar
  • 2 tsp cinnamon, ground

Place 1 1/2 cups of flour, sugar, cardamom, cinnamon, and salt in a plastic container. Heat in microwave until 110°F (20-30 sec). Add yeast, close container and shake to mix. Soften butter in microwave for 30 sec. Add milk and microwave until 110°F. In large bowl, add, milk/butter and flour mix and stir until smooth. Mix in 2 Tbsp of blended egg. Microwave the rest of the flour (1 1/2 cups) to 110°F and mix most of it into the dough. Keep adding flour until the dough is smooth...sticky but not sticking. Sprinkle with flour and cover will plastic wrap and let it rise untill double in size (30-40 min). Divide in two and flatten each part until they are about 10"x13". Spread the filling. Roll the dough into two 13" logs, seam down. Place on parchment-paper-covered baking sheet. With a sharp knife, cut the roll half-way down in 1" slices. Shape the log into a curve, seam opening under the longer side. Cover and let it rise to double in size. Brush with the rest of the egg. Sprinke with sugar and bake at 400°F for 15-20 min. Serve with coffee.

 Bourbon Chocolate Cake

  • 200 g (14 Tbsp) butter
  • bread crumbs, finely ground
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2/3 cup chocolate milk (Promissed Land brand)
  • 1 Tbsp bourbon or brandy
  • 1 2/3 cup all purpose flour
  • 2 tsp double-acting baking powder

Heat the oven to 350°F. Melt butter and let it cool. Brush the inside of a 9-inch spring-form pan with the butter and coat with the bread crumbs. Beat the eggs and sugar until light yellow and thick. Stir in butter, milk, and bourbon. Mix flour and baking powder in a separate bowl and stir in the mixture into the batter. Pour into spring-form pan and bake for about 45 min until a toothpick comes out dry. Allow to cool for 10 min and remove from pan and transfer to plate or tray. Cool completely before serving with coffee.

Originally, a dish served in Sweden each Tuesday from the Tuesday before Lent to Fat Tuesday. Traditionally, the dish was simply a piece of sweet bread served in a bowl of warm milk (hetvägg). Today, it is far more but you can still serve it in a bowl of warm milk. Go for it!
 Shrove Tuesday Buns (Semlor)

  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 2 3/4 Tbsp butter
  • 1/4 cup sugar, divided
  • 1 1/2 tsp dry yeast
  • 1 egg, sightly beaten, divided
  • 1 3/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp salt, finely ground
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom, finely ground
    Filling
  • 3 1/2 oz almond paste
  • 1 1/2 Tbsp whole milk
  • whipped cream (we use pre-whipped in a can)
  • 1 Tbsp sugar, finely ground (or powdered)

Invest in a digital kitchen thermometer for baking. Use a mortar and pestle for fine grinding. Carefully microwave the milk, butter, and 1/2 tsp of sugar until butter is melted and the temperature of the mixture is 100-110°. Stir in the yeast and let it stand for 5 min. Mix in 2 Tbsp of the egg. Mix and carefully microwave flour, remaining sugar, salt, and cardamom mixture until 100-110°F. In a bowl, mix dry and wet ingredients. Mix and knead for 4-5 minutes, adding additional flour until dough is smooth, sticky, but not sticking. Cover with plastic wrap, place in cold oven with digital thermometer, and turn on oven for 20-30 seconds. The target is to warm oven to 100-110°F. Allow dough to rise and double in size (40-60 min). Remove from oven onto a floured surface. Knead dough for 5 sec. and divide into six pieces. Roll each piece between your hands to form a ball and place on parchment-covered baking sheet. Cover with moist kitchen towel and placed in the warm oven. Allow to rise to double in size (25-30 min). Remove from oven and heat oven to 400°F. Brush rolls with the remaining egg mixed with 1/2 Tbsp water. Bake for 15-20 min. Let cool on wire rack under moist kitchen towel (30-40 min). Freeze buns you will not use. Slice off about 1/4 off the top of the bun (set top aside). Use a small fork and remove the internal part of the bun (2" diam., 1/2" deep) and save the crumbs. Mix crumbs with almond paste and milk (~1 oz almond paste and 1/2 Tbsp milk per two buns) to a smooth thick paste. Fill the hollowed out part of the bun with the paste, top with whipped cream, and place the saved bun top on top of the cream. Sprinke the top with the powdered sugar. Serve immediately with coffee. (Do not fill and decorate more buns than what you will eat in a sitting.)

 Buttery Cardamon Rolls

  • 2 1/2 Tbsp half-and-half
  • 4 tsp sugar, divided
  • 1 Tbsp dry yeast
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 3 1/2 Tbsp butter, soften
  • 1 1/4 cup flour (plus some extra)
    Filling
  • 3 1/2 Tbsp butter, soften
  • 5 Tbsp sugar
  • 2 tsp cardamon
    • For Brushing
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 2 Tbsp chopped almonds
  • 1 Tbsp coarse sugar

Invest in a digital kitchen thermometer for baking. Carefully microwave the half-and-half and 1/2 tsp of sugar until the temperature of the mixture is 100-110°. Stir in the yeast and let it stand for 5 min. Mix in the egg and remaining sugar. Carefully microwave flour and butter until 100-110°F. Use the tips of your fingers to pinch and blend in the butter into the flour until you have a crumbly mixture. Add the yeast and egg mixture. Mix and knead for 4-5 minutes, adding additional flour until dough is smooth. It will feel and look shiny and greasy. Cover with plastic wrap, place in cold oven with digital thermometer, and turn on oven for 20-30 seconds at a time. The target is to warm oven to 100-110°F. Allow dough to rise and double in size (30 min) together with a small bowl containing filling ingredients. Remove from oven and kneed dough for 30 sec. Place on a floured surface and press and strech until you have a rectange 18"x10". Spread the fillings on 2/3 of rectangle (12"x10"). Fold the 1/3 unfilled part over onto 1/2 of the filled part. Then fold over the exposed 1/3 filled part onto the other parts. You will now have a 3-layer rectange 6"x10". Slice into ten 6"x1" strips. Stretch each strip sligtly, twist and roll, tucking the end under. Place in paper muffin cups. Cover with moist kitchen towel and placed in the warm oven. Allow to rise to double in size (20 min). Remove from oven, and heat oven to 450°F. Brush rolls with the egg and sprinkle almonds and sugar on top. Bake for 8-9 min. Let rolls cool under moist kitchen towel (20-30 min). Serve with coffee (fika). (10 buns)

 Sourdough Bread

  • 3/4 cup water, divided
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 Tbsp dry instant milk
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp dry yeast
  • 2 1/2 cup (plus more) all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup starter (see below)

This is a time-consuming activity. (To create and maintain a starter, see link. Once we have a starter, we keep it (about 1/2 cup) in the fridge.) Remove starter from fridge and feed it 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour, 1/4 cup of water, and 1/2 Tbsp instant milk powder. Wait until the starter is bubbly and has doubled is size (6 hrs). Mix 1/2 cup of water, the milk powder, and salt in microwavable container. Place 1/4 cup of water and sugar in a second microwavable container. Microwave the two containers for about 20 sec until 100-110°F. Add the yeast to the 1/4 cup of water. Stir both containers. Wait 5 min. Microwave flour for about 20 sec until 100-110°F. Place flour in a bowl and make a deep hole. Add starter and both water mixtures to hole. Stir the liquid, incorporating more and more flour from the sides until it is all blended together. Knead and add more flour as needed until the dough is smooth and sticky but not sticking. Cover the bowl and place in warm (100°F) oven until double is size (45 min). Punch down the dough and knead, add more flour as needed. Make a round ball, cover with moist towel, and place on parchment-paper-covered baking sheet. Place in warm oven for 30 min. Remove from oven and heat oven to 425°F. Bake for 25-30 min. (1 loaf)

When Natalee passed way, she left behind friends, good memories, and recipies. Here is...
 Natalee's Famous Brownies

  • 2 boxes Betty Crocker Supreme Tripple Chunk Brownie Mix
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2/3 cup Crisco vegetable oil
  • 5 large eggs (room temp)
  • 2 cups Nestle's Toll House Milk Chocolate Morsels

Yes, we know about the product placement, but Natalee was very specific about the ingredients. Thus, no substitutes please! In large mixing bowl, wisk together eggs, water, and oil until well mixed. Add Mix and morsels and blend until no dry pockets are left. Pour into a greased 9x13" baking pan and bake in a 350°F oven for 50 min and a toothpick comes out dry, not longer. Remove from oven and cover pan tightly with aluminum foil to preserve moisture. On occations, Natalee allowed subtitution of the morsels with white chocolate chips or peanut butter chocolate chips. (Freezes well) Thank You, and RIP, Natalee.
