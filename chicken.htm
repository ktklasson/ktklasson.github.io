 Grape Chicken

  • 8 chicken thighs, skinned and trimmed
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 1/2 Tbsp olive oil
  • 3/4 cup white wine (cooking wine is OK)
  • 1 1/2 cup seedless green grapes, halved
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 4 Tbsp fresh parsley, chopped

Salt and pepper the chicken. Brown chicken with oil in a deep skillet until golden brown. Add wine and simmer for 10 min. Add grapes and half of the cream. Bring to boil and add remaining cream. Cover and simmer for 20 min. Top off with parsley before serving over cooked rice or cauliflower rice. (4 servings)

 Feta Cheese-Stuffed Chicken (This is a variation of a recipe from Cook's)

  • 4 chicken breasts halves, with skin and ribs
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 3/4 cup black olives, pitted
  • 1 Tbsp thyme
  • 2 oz feta cheese
  • 2 Tbsp olive oil

With a scarp knife remove ribs (and all bones) from breasts, carefully not to lose any meat. (If the "tenderloin" comes off, save it for later.) Place the rib-free breasts, skin up, on a flat surface. Process garlic in a food processor until minced. Add olives, thyme, feta cheese, and 1 Tbsp olive oil; process just until a paste is formed but not longer since you want to retain texture.
Loosen the skin on each chicken breast to form a pocket with the opening toward the meaty side. Stuff each pocket with 2 Tbsp of paste (and any tenderloin). Rub the remaining paste over the skin. Heat oven broiler. Heat to medium-high the remaining oil in an oven-safe skillet on stove. Add chicken breasts, skin sides down; sauté, turning once, until golden brown on both sides (4-5 min on each side). (Don't worry if paste sticks to the bottom of skillet.) Keep skin sides up and place skillet 2" under broiler.
Broil until chicken skin is crisp (almost burned), about 5 min longer (keep a close watch while you broil). (4 servings)

 Sweet and Spicy Chicken

  • 1 8-oz can pineapple, in chunks
  • 1 Tbsp vegetable oil
  • 3 chicken breast halves; skinless, boneless, and cubed
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 1 16-oz can cut green beans, drained
  • 3/4 cup sweet & sour sauce

Drain pineapple; reserving the juice. In skillet, heat oil and brown chicken. Stir in reserved juice and red pepper. Bring to boil, reduce heat, and cook 5 min. Add green beans, pineapple, sauce and 1/3 cup of water. Cover and cook until heated through. Serve with cooked rice or cauliflower rice. (4 small servings)

 Flying Jacob

  • 1 lb cooked rotisserie chicken, in chunks
  • 1 cup rice
  • 1 6-oz can manadrin orange segments
  • 1/4 cup salted peanuts
  • 1 banana, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 Tbsp chili powder
  • 1/2 Tbsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 3/4 cup heavy wipping cream

Cook rice according to direction, substituting some of the water for the manadrin fruit juice. Place rice in oven-safe dish. Top with chicken, peanuts, and fruit. Mix chili powder, curry, soy sauce and cream, and poor over the top. Bake at 450°F for 20 min, until bubbly. For variation, substitute chicken with pork, turkey, or ham. (4 servings)

 White Chili

  • 1 yellow onion, diced
  • 1 tsp oregano
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil
  • 4 cups water
  • 4 cups chicken stock
  • 1 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp cumin, ground
  • 3 Tbsp fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 4.5-oz can chopped green chilis
  • 1 lb cooked chicken, diced
  • 3 14.5-oz cans Great Northern beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 cup corn starch
  • 1/2 cup cold water

In a large pot, saute onion and oregano in olive oil until soft. Add the next eight ingredients. Bring to boil and simmer for 15 min. Mix corn starch and cold water; add to chili; continue to simmer until the chili thickens, then remove from heat. Serve with sour cream, Monterey Jack cheese, and green chili hot sauce. (8 servings)

 Rosemary Chicken

  • 8 chicken thighs, skin and fat removed
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 Tbsp black pepper, cracked
  • 1/2 Tbsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 Tbsp thyme
  • 1/2 Tbsp oregano
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 cup canola oil
  • juice of 1/2 lemon
  • 1/2 cup packed fresh rosemary
  • 1 4.5-oz jar garlic
  • 3/4 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup dry white wine
  • 1.5 lb cauliflower, frozen

In bowl, mix ingredients for the marinade (salt through garlic). Place chicken in 1-gallon zip-lock bag and pour over the marinade. Squeeze out the air, seal the bag, massage the bag until the marinade and chicken pieces are well mixed, and place the bag in the fridge overnight.
Preheat oven to 500°F. In large skillet over medium-high heat, drain enough marinade to coat the bottom. Transfer the chicken from the bag to the skillet, leaving most of the marinade and and rosemary in the bag. Brown chichen on both sides until almost cooked through (about 8-10 min). Arrange chicken in 9"x13" deep pan, pour over the remaining marinade, chicken broth, and wine. Roast in the oven for 10 min. Add the frozen cauliflower on top of the chicken and roast for 20 min longer. If the cauliflower is not browed, switch the oven to broil and move up the rack in the oven for a couple of minutes. Remove the dish from the oven and let is rest for about 5 min before serving. No need to add any sides, unless you want to. (4 servings)

 Mustard Chicken

  • 14 oz Creme fraiche
  • 4 oz bacon, finely chopped
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper, ground

 Turkey Poblano Gumbo

  • 1 Tbps salt
  • 2 tsp basil
  • 2 tsp paprika
  • 1 1/2 tsp dry mustard
  • 1 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1 1/4 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp Cayenne pepper
  • 1 tsp chervil
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp white pepper
  • 1/2 tsp thyme
  • 1 1/4 lb boneless turkey breast, cut in 1/2-inch pieces
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 cup dry roux
  • 3 cups poblano peppers, cut in 1/2-inch pieces
  • 2 cups onion, chopped
  • 1 cup green bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 cup yellow bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 cup red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 lb andouille sausage, quartered lengthwise and cut in 1/2-inch pieces
  • 6 cups chicken broth

(Note that this is a very spicy dish; you may want to leave out the Cayenne pepper the first time you try it.) Blend all spices in a small bowl. Coat turkey with 4 tsp of the spieces and set aside. In large deep pan, heat oil over medium-high heat. Add 2/3 cup of the dry roux and stir constantly until dissolved and bubbly. Add remaining spice mix, stir, and then add the vegetables and sausage. Stir well, reduce to medium heat, and cover. Cook for 18 min, uncovering and stirring/scraping every 2-3 min to prevent burning. Add 3 cups chicken stock, the remaining dry roux, and the seasoned turkey meat. Increase the heat until boiling. Cook for 8 min, stirring/scraping every 1 min. Add remainig chicken stock. Increase the heat until boiling then reduce to simmer for 15 min. Serve over cooked rice or cauliflower rice. (8 servings)

 Coq Au Vin

  • 8 skinless chicken thighs
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp ground black
  • 8 oz bacon, sliced crosswise 1/2-inch wide (scissors)
  • 10 large button mushrooms, quartered
  • 1/2 large yellow onion, chopped
  • 2 green onions, sliced (white + green parts)
  • 1 Tbsp butter
  • 2 tsp flour
  • 1 1/2 cups red whine (we use alcohol free, Fre)
  • 1 1/2 tsp dry thyme leaves
  • 1 cup chicken broth

Season the chicken thights with salt and pepper, set aside. In deep skillet or pan, cook bacon over medium-high heat until evenly brown and beginning to crisp. Remove with slotted spoon and cool on paper towel. In the same skillet, over medium-high heat, brown chicken on both sides for about 8 min. Remove and set aside. Discard all but 1 Tbsp of drippings. Over medium-high heat, cook mushrooms, onion, and green onion with a pinch of salt until golden and caramelized, about 10 min. Add butter and then flour. Add wine and bring to boil while scraping the botton of pan until clean. Add bacon and thyme. Simmer for 5 min. Add chicken and chicken broth. Simmer for 30 min. Serve over cooked rice or cauliflower rice. (4 servings)

 Crock Pot Chicken

  • 1.5 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1-oz package Ranch seasoning mix
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth
  • 8 oz cream cheese
  • 8 oz bacon, cooked and crumbled
  • 8 green onion tops, slized
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • sweet bread buns for serving

Cook the four first ingedients in a slow cooker for 3 hrs on high, or 5-6 hrs on low, until the chicken is tender and can easily be shredded. Using two forks, shredd the chicken and mix in the rest of the ingredients. Serve on sweet rolls as sliders. (8 servings)

 Jambalaya

  • 2 1/2 Tblsp butter, divided
  • 1/4 lb Chorizo sausage
  • 1 cup yellow onion, chopped
  • 1 cup celery, chopped
  • 3 green onion, sliced
  • 4 oz can mushroom pieces, drained
  • 1/2 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 lb Andouille smoked sausage, cut in half length-wise, and sliced
  • 1/4 lb tasso, cut in 1/4 pieces
  • 1 tsp Creole seasoning, salt-free
  • 2 1/2 cup uncooked rice
  • 1 lb cooked rotissary chicken, deskinned, deboned, chopped
  • 1 can Rotel diced tomatoes and green chilies
  • 1 can Campbell's beef broth
  • 1 can Campbell's French onion soup
  • 1 1/2 cup chicken stock
  • 2 tsp thyme
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 bay leaf

A lot of ingredients but worthwhile. You can do a lot of prepwork the day before. In a large (5 Qt.) pot or skillet, brown and break up the chorizo sausage in 1 Tblsp butter on medium high heat. Add the next nine ingredients and cook, while stirring, for 15-20 min until the vegetbles are soft. Add rice and stir for 3 min. Stir in the rest of the ingredients. Allow it to begin bubbling, slice and add the remaining butter. Cover, lower the heat, and allow to cook for 25 min until the rice is done. Remove from the heat, uncover, stir the mixture, cover, and let it rest for 10 min. (at least 12 servings)

 Faux Turtle Soup

  • 6 Tblsp butter, divided
  • 1 1/4 lb ground turkey breast
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper, ground
  • 1 cup yellow onion, chopped
  • 1 cup celery, chopped
  • 1 1/2 cup green and red bell pepper, chopped
  • 5 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 Tblsp thyme
  • 1/2 Tblsp oregano
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 quart beef stock
  • 1/2 cup dry roux
  • 1/2 bottle (1.5 cups) dry sherry
  • 1/2 Tblsp hot sauce
  • 2 Tblsp Worcestershire sauce
  • juice from 1 lemon
  • 1 can petite diced tomatoes (well drained)
  • 5 oz fresh spinach (stems removed), chopped
  • 3 hard-boiled eggs, chopped

This is very time consuming soup. Brown the meat in 2 Tblsp butter over medium-high heat, breaking it up into very small pieces. Season with salt and pepper and cook for about 18-20 min. Add the fresh vegetables and spices, reduce the heat to medium and cook, stirring often, for 20 min. Add the stock, increase the heat to boiling, then reduce to a simmer for 30 min. When the time is almost done, melt the remaining butter in a separate pot over medium heat and add the dry roux, stirring constantly for 3 min. Remove from heat and add liquid from the meat and vegetable pot to the roux, stirring constantly. Continue until your roux has the consistency of sour cream, then add the roux to the bigger pot a little at the time, stirring to avoid lumps. Simmer to about 25 min, stirring to avoid sticking. Add sherry and bring to boil. Add sauces, lemon juice, and tomatoes. Bring to a simmer. Add spinach and eggs and bring to simmer. Remove from heat and serve with buttered toasted (sourdough) bread. (at least 12 servings)

 Mexican Chicken

  • 1 can cream of chicken soup
  • 2 8-oz pkg of shredded Mexican blend cheese
  • 8 oz sour cream
  • 4-oz can of diced green chilies
  • 2 cups of cooked rotissary chicken, shredded
  • 1 envelope of taco seasoning
  • 6 large tortillas
  • 1 large tomato, chopped

Mix soup, 1 pkg of cheese, sour cream, chilies, chicken, and seasoning in a bowl. Spread mixture (~1/2 cup) on a tortilla and fold like a burrito. Place, fold side down, in greased 9x13" glass baking dish. Repeat with the other tortillas. Spread the rest of the filling on top, then the tomatoes, and the last pkg of cheese. Bake at 350°F for about 25 min. Let it rest for 5 min and serve with Mexican rice. (6 servings)
