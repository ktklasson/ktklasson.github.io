 Colorful Stew

  • 2 lb pork tenderloin, sliced 3/4" thick
  • 1/2 lb hot Italian sausages, sliced 1/2" thick
  • 1 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp marjoram
  • 1 Tbsp flour
  • 6 1/2 oz jar cocktail onions
  • 8 oz can black olives, halved
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced thin
  • 1/2 cup Polish gherkins pickles, cut in pieces
  • 2 cups heavy cream (or half-and-half)

Flatten tenderloin slices with your hand. Brown sausage without oil in a deep skillet over medium heat; when brown, remove sausage and brown tenderloin in leftover fat. Add salt, pepper, and marjoram. Sprinkle over flour and stir. Add onions, but save the liquid. Also add olives, pickles, bell pepper, sausages, and cream. Let the mixture come to a boil and simmer for 15 min. Add reserved "onion liquid" and heat through. Serve with cooked rice, a side salad, and a good Shiraz wine. (6 servings.)

 Pork with Curry and Banana

  • 1 lb pork tenderloin, sliced 1-inch thick
  • 1 Tbsp butter
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/8 tsp white pepper, ground
  • 2 bananas; sliced lengthwise, then in quarters
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 2 Tbsp curry powder
  • 1/2 cup Mozzarella cheese, shredded

Flatten pork slices slightly; salt and pepper both sides. Brown pork with butter in oven-safe skillet. Add bananas and cream mixed with curry powder. Sprinkle cheese on top; bake in oven 450°F, 10 min. Serve with boiled rice and a Chardonnay. (4 servings.)

 Frontier Beans

  • 1/2 lb chorizo sausage
  • 8 green onions, chopped
  • 3 Anaheim chilies; roasted, peeled, and chopped
  • 2 16-oz cans pinto beans, 1 can drained
  • 1 large tomato, chopped
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Heat oven to 350°F. In cast iron skillet, brown sausage and onion until done. Stir in the remaining ingredients. Bake uncovered for 30 min. Serve with flour tortillas, corn chips, and a salad. Tastes great with your favorite beer or milk. (4 servings)

 Pork with Fruit and Peppers

  • 3 lb pork tenderloin, sliced 3/4" thick
  • 3 Tbsp salt-free Creole seasoning
  • 1.5 tsp salt
  • 3 Tbsp flour
  • 4 Tbsp butter
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, sliced lengthwise
  • 1/2 yellow bell pepper, sliced lenghtwise
  • 1/4 cup brandy
  • 2 cloves
  • 3 peaches, cut in segments (or you can use frozed pre-cut, 16 oz)
  • 1/2 cup late-harvest white wine
  • 1/2 tsp Tabasco sauce

Combine seasoning, salt and flour, and coat the pork with the mixture. Heat half of the butter in a deep skillet over medium/high heat and saute peppers (~ 5 min). Remove peppers and set aside. Add the rest of the butter and brown pork (you may have to do this in a couple of batches) on both sides (~8 min). Remove pork and set aside. Add brandy to pan let most of it evaporate as you stir to loosen up what ever is stuck at the bottom of the pan. Add 1/2 cup of water, cloves, peaches, and pork. Simmer for 20 min, then remove the pork. Simmer the sauce for an additional 5 min. Remove the cloves and discard. Add wine and Tabasco and use a stick blender to process the sauce until smooth. Add pork and peppers and heat for about 5 min. If sauce is to thick, add some more water. Serve with cooked rice and a nice white wine. (8 servings)

 Red Beans and Rice

  • 1 lb red kidney beans
  • 1 yellow onion, chopped
  • 2 Tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 lb tasso (major fat removed), cut in bean-sized cubes
  • 1/2 lb Andouille sausage, sliced lenght-wise then in 1/4-inch half-moons
  • 1 Tbsp garlic, chopped
  • 2 Tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 1 tsp black pepper, ground
  • 1 tsp salt
  • cooked rice, for serving

Pick through the beans and remove unwanted particles. Wash beans in water and bring to a boil together with the onion with 2 quarts of water in a large Dutch oven or pot. Reduce heat, cover, and boil slowly for 1 hr. With slotted spoon, remove about 2 cups of cooked beans and mash them well with a fork in a bowl. Heat oil in frying pan over medium-high heat. Add tasso and sausage and saute for about 5 min. Transfer tasso and sausage to bowl of mashed beans and remove about a cup of liquid from the large pot and deglaze the pan. Return liquid, tasso, sausage, mashed beans to the large pot. Add garlic, parsley, and spices and simmer for about 30 more minutes. (This is a good time to prepare the rice.) Serve over cooked rice (and hot sauce on the side) with Chianti or an amber ale. (8 servings)

 Chorizo Sausage

  • 2 lb ground pork or pork/beef mix
  • 4 Tbsp chili powder
  • 2 Tbsp paprika, ground
  • 2 Tbsp oregano, dry
  • 1 tsp cumin, ground
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/8 tsp cinnamon, ground
  • 1/8 tsp cloves, ground
  • 1/2 cup vinegar

Mix all the ingredients with your hands (use gloves) in a large bowl. Refrigerate for at least one hour. Use as is or form 1-inch balls and freeze for later use.

 Paprika Pork

  • 2 Tbsp butter
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper, ground
  • 1/2 tsp Cayenne pepper, ground
  • 1 onion, quartered and sliced thick
  • 2 lb pork tenderloin, sliced and halved
  • 1 1/2 Tbsp paprika
  • 1 cup chicken broth, divided
  • 2 1/2 Tbsp flour
  • 2 cups full-fat sour cream

Cook salt, pepper, onion, and butter in a large deep skillet for 5 min. Add paprika and pork and stir until no pink meat is seen. Add 1/2 cup of chicken stock and let it simmer for 5 min. With a slotted spoon, remove pork and onion. Mix flour with remaining chicken broth and add to skillet. Wisk until boiling. Add sour cream and wisk until the mix is uniform. Add back the pork and onion and heat until hot. Serve over egg noodles. (6 servings)

 Quiche Lorraine

  • 1 pie shell
  • 6 slices of bacon
  • 1/2 yellow onion, diced (~2/3 cup)
  • 3 Tblsp green peper, diced
  • 1 cup fresh mushrooms, chopped
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1/8 tsp of salt
  • 1/8 tsp of garlic powder
  • 1/8 tsp of white pepper
  • 1/4 tsp of hot sauce (Tabasco, Crystal)
  • 1 1/2 cup Swiss cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup mild Cheddar cheese, shredded

Bake pie shell for 10 min at 350°C. Cook bacon over medium heat until crisp. Let it dry on a paper towels. Saute the onion, pepper and mushrooms in the left-over bacon grease until soft. Beat the eggs for about 2 min and mix in the sour cream and spices. Add the bacon, vegetables, and cheeses. Pour into the pie shell and bake for 35-40 min at 350°C until slightly brown on top. Remove from oven and allow it to rest for 10 min before serving with a salad. (6 portions)
